# Marvel Legendary — Icon & Card Layout Reference

Descriptions derived from direct visual inspection of the reference PNG images in this folder. For edge cases or uncertain identifications, the original PNG files are available here as a fallback — see **Part 9** for the file list.

---

## Part 1: Class Icons

Five class types exist. Every hero card belongs to exactly one class, shown as a small icon in the upper-left corner of the card (directly below the team icon). The same icon also appears embedded inline in Superpower effect text as a trigger marker.

---

### STRENGTH

**Shape:** A clenched fist depicted from a front-right angle, as if mid-punch toward the viewer. Chunky, cartoonish proportions. The fingers fold over the thumb. Three or four short diagonal speed-lines extend from the upper-left of the fist, indicating motion/impact.
**Color:** Vivid medium green — clearly saturated, not olive, not dark forest green, not neon lime. A clean mid-range green, vibrant and immediately readable.
**Distinguishing features:**
- The ONLY class icon depicting a body part (a fist/hand)
- The ONLY green class icon
- The motion speed-lines behind the fist are a reliable secondary indicator
**Cannot be confused with:** Any other class icon — the fist shape in green is unique.

---

### TECH

**Shape:** A square/rectangular icon (roughly equal-proportion square) with a printed circuit board (PCB) pattern. The bottom edge has a row of small rectangular pins/tabs, like the leads on an integrated circuit chip or microchip. The interior shows circuit traces: small circular nodes connected by right-angle horizontal and vertical lines. The overall impression is strongly of a computer microchip or motherboard component.
**Color:** Gray / silver-gray throughout. No bright colors. Slightly metallic in appearance. This is the only class icon with no warm or cool color — it is neutral gray.
**Distinguishing features:**
- The ONLY class icon with a SQUARE/RECTANGULAR outer shape (all others are organic, rounded, or freeform)
- The ONLY gray class icon
- The PCB trace pattern (nodes + right-angle lines) is unmistakeable
**Cannot be confused with:** Any other class icon — the square shape and neutral gray color are unique.

---

### COVERT

**Shape:** Two curved arrows forming a circular rotation symbol — like a two-arrow "refresh" or "recycle" symbol, but with two opposing arrows instead of three. Each arrow curves approximately 180°, bending from one side to the other. The arrowheads point in opposite directions, creating a sense of continuous circular motion. The overall outline IS circular, but it is formed by the two arrow bodies — it is NOT a solid ring with arrows attached; the arrows themselves make the circle.
**Color:** Deep red / dark crimson. Not bright red. Not orange-red. A darker, muted, desaturated red — closer to the color of dried blood or dark burgundy than a fire engine.
**Distinguishing features:**
- The ONLY class icon with a rotation/cycling arrow design
- The ONLY red class icon (the Attack slash icon is also red but is a completely different shape — see Part 3)
- The circular-but-open construction (arrows, not a solid ring) is distinctive
**Cannot be confused with:** Any class icon — the dark red circular rotation is unique. (Note: the ATTACK value icon is also red, but consists of three straight parallel slashes, not curved arrows in a circle.)

---

### INSTINCT

**Shape:** A roughly hexagonal badge or bottlecap shape — an irregular polygon slightly wider than tall, with a slightly flat top and a flat bottom edge. Similar to a police badge outline, a bottlecap, or a rounded shield with six approximate sides. Inside the badge shape are three short vertical parallel marks or ridges, centered within the badge, slightly darker than the surrounding fill.
**Color:** Outer border/outline is black or very dark brown. Fill is solid orange/amber — a warm orange-yellow tone, the color of an orange traffic cone, an amber gemstone, or tiger's eye. This is distinctly orange, with clear red-warmth to it.
**Distinguishing features:**
- The ONLY class icon with a badge/bottlecap polygon shape
- The ONLY orange/amber class icon
- The three interior ridge marks are a secondary identifier
**Color calibration note:** The orange fill is warmer and more orange-red than the RECRUIT star (which is golden-yellow, closer to pure yellow with less red in it). When uncertain about orange vs. gold, look at the shape — badge/polygon = Instinct; five-pointed star = Recruit.
**Cannot be confused with:** Any other class icon — the orange badge polygon is unique.

---

### RANGED

**Shape:** A circular targeting reticle or crosshair. NOT a solid circle — the outer boundary is formed by four triangular/pointed arc-segments arranged at 12, 3, 6, and 9 o'clock positions, with visible gaps between the segments. The effect is like a four-pointed compass rose or targeting sight. A small central dot or node appears at dead center. The overall impression is of a sniper scope or weapon targeting reticle.
**Color:** Light blue / cyan / sky blue — a cool, clear, bright blue. Not navy, not royal blue, not teal. A distinctly light, airy blue that is immediately distinguishable from darker blues.
**Distinguishing features:**
- The ONLY blue class icon
- The four pointed arc-segments with gaps between them (not a solid circle ring)
- The crosshair/targeting design association
**CRITICAL confusion warning — CRIME SYNDICATE team icon:** Both RANGED and CRIME SYNDICATE are circular crosshair-style designs. RANGED is BLUE/CYAN with four pointed arc-segments separated by gaps. CRIME SYNDICATE is GOLD/YELLOW with straight cross-lines (horizontal + vertical) through a solid circle outline. **Color is the primary differentiator: blue/cyan = Ranged class; gold/yellow = Crime Syndicate team.** When you see a circular crosshair, check color immediately.

## Part 2: Team Icons

Every hero card shows a team affiliation icon in the upper-left corner, ABOVE the class icon. The same icon appears embedded inline in Superpower effect text as a trigger. A hero with no team affiliation (e.g., Silver Surfer from the Fantastic Four set) has an empty team slot — this is intentional and correct; do not flag as missing data.

---

### AVENGERS

**Shape:** A circular badge/medallion with a stylized bold letter "A" centered inside.
**Color:** The circle background is a warm orange-red gradient (the color palette of the Iron Man/Avengers movie logo — a fiery, warm orange fading toward red). The "A" itself is rendered in warm gold/yellow against the orange-red. The outer circle border is also warm-toned.
**Distinguishing features:** Contains a prominent letter "A." One of the few team icons using a text character as the primary design. The warm orange-red + gold color combination is unique among team icons.
**Cannot be confused with:** Any other team icon — the letter "A" on a warm orange-red circle is unmistakeable.

---

### S.H.I.E.L.D.

**Shape:** A circular badge with a spread eagle in heraldic style — wings wide open, body facing forward, in the classic heraldic "displayed" eagle pose.
**Color:** Dark gray/charcoal circular background. Eagle rendered in silver/white.
**Distinguishing features:** The ONLY team icon featuring a bird. The dark gray background + silver eagle combination is unique.
**Cannot be confused with:** Any other icon — no other Legendary icon features an eagle or any bird.

---

### X-MEN

**Shape:** A circular badge with a clean, geometric letter "X" centered inside. The X has straight, equal-width arms — not tapered, not textured, not angled. A distinct circular border ring is visible around the badge.
**Color:** The interior background of the circle is **red**. The X is **black**. The outer circular border/ring is also **black**.
**Distinguishing features:** Clean, smooth, black X on a red background, enclosed in a circular badge with a black border ring.
**CRITICAL confusion warning — three X variant icons:**
- **X-MEN**: Circular badge + red interior + black X + black border ring
- **X-FORCE**: Circular badge present (red circle) + gold/yellow X on top
- **X-FACTOR**: NO circular badge — blue X with angular pointed tips at the ends of each arm
Color and presence/absence of the circular badge are the key differentiators. Check all three: shape of X tips (clean vs. angular vs. textured), color (dark+white vs. gold vs. blue), and whether a circular badge surrounds it.

---

### X-FORCE

**Shape:** A gold/yellow X centered on a red circle — similar format to X-Men but with different colors. The X may have some diagonal line styling that gives it slight visual weight.
**Color:** Gold/yellow X on a red circular background.
**Distinguishing features:** Gold/yellow X on red circle. Compared to X-Men: same basic circular-badge format, but colors are reversed (X-Men is black X on red; X-Force is gold X on red).
**See X-MEN entry above for full three-way X variant comparison.**
**Note:** Both X-Men and X-Force use a circular badge with a red background — differentiate by the X color: black = X-Men, gold = X-Force.

---

### X-FACTOR

**Shape:** A standalone "X" shape without a circular badge. The ends/tips of each arm of the X taper to sharp angular points, giving the X a claw-like or lightning-bolt quality at the tips. More angular and aggressive-looking than the clean X-Men X.
**Color:** Medium blue / royal blue.
**Distinguishing features:** Blue color (unique among all three X variants). Angular pointed tips.
**Confusion warnings:**
- vs. other X variants: See X-MEN entry above
- vs. RANGED class icon: Both are blue. RANGED is a CIRCULAR CROSSHAIR shape with pointed arc segments; X-FACTOR is a letter X shape with angular tips. Shape distinguishes them easily.

---

### SPIDER-FRIENDS

**Shape:** A circular icon with a spider silhouette centered inside. The spider has an elongated ovoid body with eight legs radiating outward in the classic Spider-Man chest emblem style.
**Color:** Red circle background. Black spider silhouette.
**Distinguishing features:** The ONLY icon depicting a spider. Unmistakeable.
**Cannot be confused with:** Any other icon — no other Legendary icon features a spider or insect.

---

### MARVEL KNIGHTS

**Shape:** A text/typographic logo showing the letters "MK" in a stylized bold font. Not a pictograph or symbol — letters are the entire design.
**Color:** Red letters with a dark/black outline or shadow treatment.
**Distinguishing features:** The ONLY team icon that uses text/letters as the primary design rather than a symbol. "MK" is clearly legible.
**Cannot be confused with:** Any other icon — no other team icon is purely typographic.

---

### FANTASTIC FOUR

**Shape:** A circular badge with a large bold numeral "4" centered inside, displayed within or alongside a square outline inside the circle. The "4" is large and fills most of the badge area.
**Color:** Blue background circle. White "4" and white square.
**Distinguishing features:** The ONLY icon containing a numeral. Blue and white circular badge. Classic FF logo.
**Cannot be confused with:** Any other icon — the numeral "4" is completely unique.

---

### GUARDIANS OF THE GALAXY

**Shape:** An oval or slightly irregular circle (noticeably non-circular — wider than tall or with an oval quality) showing a space/galaxy scene. Has a distinct astronomical appearance: a brighter central core area with swirling darker forms suggesting spiral galaxy arms or nebula clouds extending outward. Gives the impression of a bird's-eye view of a spiral galaxy.
**Color:** Blue-purple space palette. The center is lighter/brighter; the outer areas are darker with swirling shapes. Has a distinctly "cosmic/space" aesthetic.
**Distinguishing features:** The ONLY icon depicting an astronomical or space scene. The oval-ish shape and the swirling galaxy imagery together are unmistakeable.
**Cannot be confused with:** Any other icon — the space/galaxy scene is unique in the game.

---

### HEROES OF ASGARD

**Shape:** A circular badge showing Thor's hammer (Mjolnir). The hammer has its characteristic broad square head on a short handle with a leather strap at the bottom. May be shown at a slight angle within the circle.
**Color:** Gray/silver hammer head, brown/dark handle. The circular background is light or has a metallic/shiny quality.
**Distinguishing features:** The ONLY icon depicting a hammer or any weapon/tool.
**Cannot be confused with:** Any other icon — Mjolnir's distinctive shape is unique.

---

### WARBOUND

**Shape:** A circular icon with what appears to be a rough, rocky or armored face or figure inside. The imagery is somewhat organic and detailed rather than a clean geometric design.
**Color:** Greenish-gray / olive green tones. Somewhat murky/dark. The color palette evokes armor, rock, or the Hulk's green-gray skin.
**Distinguishing features:** The greenish-gray (Hulk-like) color within a circular format.
**Note on small-size readability:** This icon can be difficult to read at small card sizes due to the detailed, dark imagery. If uncertain at small size, verify via DB or PDF rather than guessing. The greenish-gray color is the primary identifier — compare against CHAMPIONS (stark black + gold ring) and CRIME SYNDICATE (gold crosshair) which are both circular but very different in color.
**Fallback:** Read `Icons_p-4.png` for a larger view if needed.

---

### CHAMPIONS

**Shape:** A circular icon with a stark, high-contrast two-tone design: a large solid black circle filling the center, surrounded by a golden/amber outer ring.
**Color:** Black center, golden/amber ring. Very high contrast between the two zones.
**Distinguishing features:** The stark BLACK + GOLD ring contrast makes this one of the most visually recognizable icons at any size. The black center is solid and bold.
**Cannot be confused with:** Any other icon — no other icon has a black-circle-in-gold-ring composition.

---

### CRIME SYNDICATE

**Shape:** A circular targeting reticle / gunsight with straight crosshair lines (one horizontal, one vertical) plus an inner concentric circle ring — making it look like a proper multi-ring gunsight rather than just a plain cross in a circle. The lines are straight (not angled, not pointed arcs).
**Color:** Gold / amber / warm yellow. Consistent throughout.
**Distinguishing features:** Gold color + multi-ring gunsight design (outer circle, inner circle ring, and straight cross lines).
**CRITICAL confusion warning — RANGED class icon:** Both icons are circular crosshair designs. The ONLY reliable differentiator is color: **RANGED is BLUE/CYAN; CRIME SYNDICATE is GOLD/YELLOW.** Additionally: RANGED has four pointed arc-segment shapes with gaps between them; CRIME SYNDICATE has straight cross-lines (like a plus sign) through a solid circle outline. When you see a circular crosshair, identify the color immediately.

---

### CABAL

**Shape:** A downward-pointing triangle or downward-pointing shield/chevron shape. May contain interior design elements (possibly a circular or sun-like motif), but the triangular outline is the primary shape.
**Color:** Purple / violet.
**Distinguishing features:** DOWNWARD-pointing triangle (unique — the only downward-pointing triangular icon in the game). Purple color (unique among all icons).
**Cannot be confused with:** ILLUMINATI (which is upward-pointing triangle, golden yellow, with an eye inside) — the orientation (down vs. up), color (purple vs. gold), and interior design are all different.

---

### ILLUMINATI

**Shape:** An upward-pointing equilateral triangle (flat base at bottom, single point at top). The triangle is filled entirely with color.
**Color:** Bright golden yellow / amber filling the entire triangle.
**Interior detail:** Centered inside the triangle is the "Eye of Providence" / "All-Seeing Eye" — an open human eye (showing iris, pupil, and both eyelids) surrounded by a ring of small dark circular dots or beads (like a dotted halo or rosary beads encircling the eye). This eye-with-dotted-ring motif is distinctive and recognizable.
**Distinguishing features:** Upward-pointing triangle in bright gold + the all-seeing eye with dotted ring inside.
**Cannot be confused with:**
- CABAL: Downward-pointing triangle, purple color, no eye
- Any other icon: The triangle + eye + dotted ring combination is unique in the game

## Part 3: Recruit & Attack Icons

These appear in two contexts: (1) in the lower-left corner of hero cards as base value indicators, and (2) embedded inline in effect text when an ability grants recruit or attack points.

---

### RECRUIT ★

**Shape:** A classic five-pointed star with the top point facing upward. Symmetrical, standard star proportions.
**Color:** Gold / golden yellow — a warm but clean yellow-gold. More yellow than orange.
**Context:** Appears next to the base recruit value in the lower-left of hero cards. When you see a number with a star next to it in the lower-left, that number is the card's base Recruit value.
**Color calibration vs. INSTINCT class icon:** Both are gold/warm yellow tones. RECRUIT is a FIVE-POINTED STAR shape. INSTINCT is a BADGE/BOTTLECAP polygon shape. Shape is the definitive distinguisher.

---

### ATTACK (red slashes)

**Shape:** Three parallel diagonal slash marks, angled from upper-right to lower-left at approximately 45°. Like three parallel claw scratches or cut marks. Each slash has visible width — these are bold marks, not hairlines.
**Color:** Dark red / crimson. Same red family as the COVERT class icon, but the shape is completely different (straight parallel slashes vs. two rotating curved arrows).
**Context:** Appears next to the base attack value in the lower-left of hero cards. When you see a number with red slashes next to it in the lower-left, that number is the card's base Attack value.
**Cannot be confused with:** COVERT class icon — slashes are three straight parallel lines at one angle; Covert is two curved arrows forming a rotation circle.

## Part 4: Hero Card Layout

```
┌─────────────────────────────────┐
│ [TEAM ICON]                     │
│ [CLASS ICON]     CARD NAME      │
│                                 │
│  SPECIAL ABILITY text (always   │
│  fires when card is played)     │
│                                 │
│  [TRIGGER ICON]: SUPERPOWER     │
│  text (conditional effect)      │
│                                 │
│ [#]★ or [#]⚡            [#]  │
│  (Base Value)           (Cost)  │
└─────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Upper-left corner — stacked vertically:**
- **TOP position (uppermost):** Team affiliation icon — small badge or logo for the hero's team
- **BOTTOM position (directly below team icon, no gap or text between them):** Class icon — Strength fist, Tech circuit board, Covert rotation arrows, Instinct badge, or Ranged crosshair
- Both icons are small and stacked vertically. Team is ALWAYS above Class.
- **If no team affiliation:** The team slot is visually empty (confirmed case: Silver Surfer, Fantastic Four set). Do not flag this as an error or missing data.

**Card name:** Prominently displayed in a title area.

**Body text area:**
- **SPECIAL ABILITY line(s):** No icon prefix. This effect fires unconditionally every time the card is played, regardless of what else has been played this turn.
- **SUPERPOWER line(s):** Begins with a small inline class or team icon (the same icon that appears in the upper-left corner, but rendered at approximately capital-letter height within the text block), followed immediately by a colon (:). This effect ONLY fires if a card of the matching class or team was played earlier this turn.
- Multiple trigger icons in sequence (e.g., two Guardians icons side by side) means multiple qualifying cards must have been played.

**Lower-left corner:**
- The base value this card provides when played
- Shown as a number alongside either ★ (Recruit) or red slashes (Attack)
- "0" with no star/slashes: the card provides no base value; all value derives from the ability text

**Lower-right corner:**
- **Cost** — the Recruit points the player must spend to acquire this card from HQ
- Shown as a number in a visually distinct box, circle, or styled treatment that differentiates it from the lower-left base value

## Part 5: Villain & Henchmen Card Layout

**Upper-left corner:** VP value (Victory Points earned when this villain is defeated) — shown as a number.

**Card title:** Name prominently displayed.

**Card body — labeled effect lines:**
- Optional always-on keyword or passive effect written as a standalone line with no colon label (e.g., "Last Stand." or "Dark Memories.") — appears before the labeled effects
- **"Ambush:"** — effect that triggers when this card is drawn and revealed from the Villain Deck
- **"Fight:"** — effect that triggers when the player defeats this villain
- **"Escape:"** — effect that triggers when this villain escapes past the city

**Lower-right corner:** Fight value — the total Attack points required to defeat this villain.

**Henchmen:** Same general layout as villains. Group name + individual card name. Fight value. May have Fight effects; often simpler than villains.

**Location cards (some expansions, e.g., Revelations):** May use **"Ongoing:"** instead of "Fight:" for a persistent passive effect while the card is in the city, plus a "Fight:" for the effect when defeated.

---

## Part 6: Mastermind Card Layout

**Normal version:**
- Name prominently displayed
- "Always Leads:" — the villain group this mastermind requires to be in play
- "Master Strike:" — effect that triggers when Master Strike cards are revealed
- Fight value and VP value displayed

**Epic variant:** Same layout, higher stats, may have a modified Master Strike. Labeled "Epic [Name]."

**Tactic cards:** Separate smaller cards, each with a "Fight:" effect describing what happens when that tactic is defeated.

## Part 7: Attack & Recruit Value Notation

| Notation | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `3` | Fixed base value of exactly 3, always provided when the card is played |
| `0` | Provides no value of this type; all value comes from ability text |
| `—` | Stat type not applicable to this card type (e.g., villains don't have Recruit) |
| `0+` | Base is 0; ability text grants additional value conditionally |
| `3+` | Base of 3, plus a conditional bonus from ability text (the `+` signals additional value is possible) |
| `[N]*` | Asterisk on a value indicates a special combination or multiplication condition. **The card's specific effect text defines the exact rule.** Common uses include: combining attack and recruit into a single shared pool, doubling a value under certain conditions, or deriving a value from a counted quantity. Always record the card's effect text alongside the asterisk notation — never record just the asterisk without the accompanying condition. |

**When both attack and recruit appear on the same card:** The lower-left corner shows the primary base value. Secondary or conditional values are described in the effect text.

## Part 8: Inline Trigger Icons in Effect Text

**This is the most error-prone aspect of reading card images.**

Trigger icons appear INLINE within the body text of Superpower effects, embedded within the paragraph at approximately capital-letter height — the same icons described in Parts 1 and 2, but rendered at text size (very small). They are NOT in the card corners; they are mid-text.

**Why this is difficult:**
- At card print size (or scanned image size), these inline icons are approximately the same height as a capital letter
- Multiple icons can appear consecutively (e.g., two Avengers icons = "[AVENGERS][AVENGERS]:")
- The icon must be identified by both color and shape at very small scale

**Reading strategy — check these in order:**
1. **Color first** — the most reliable feature at small sizes:
   - Green = Strength
   - Gray square = Tech
   - Dark red rotation arrows = Covert
   - Orange/amber badge = Instinct
   - Light blue/cyan crosshair = Ranged (not Crime Syndicate — see below)
   - Warm orange-red circle with "A" = Avengers
   - Dark circle with silver eagle = S.H.I.E.L.D.
   - Gold circle with "4" = Fantastic Four
   - Space/oval = Guardians
   - Etc. — use the descriptions in Parts 1 and 2
2. **Shape as confirmation** — once color is identified, verify with shape
3. **Cross-reference with upper-left corner** — a Superpower trigger that matches the card's own class or team icon (shown in the upper-left) is the most common pattern; if the card's class is Strength (green fist) and you see a green inline icon, that's strong confirmation

**When uncertain:** Write `[___]` as a placeholder. Do NOT guess based on what "makes sense" for the character or what ability seems logical. It is always better to flag multiple `[___]` entries for human review than to record one incorrect trigger icon.
