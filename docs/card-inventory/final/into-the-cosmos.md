## New Keywords

- **Shards**: Tokens that can be gained by players, Villains, and the Mastermind. Unlimited supply (no cap — overrides GotG rule). Players: spend 1 Shard for +1 Attack, or save for later. Villains: gain +1 Attack per Shard; after defeat, player takes 1 Shard, rest return to supply. Mastermind: gains +1 Attack per Shard; after you fight it, take 1 Shard, rest return.
- **Burn N Shards**: Once this turn, spend exactly N Shards to do the listed effect. Does NOT give the normal +1 Attack per Shard; the Shards are consumed for the specific effect only.
- **Danger Sense N**: Reveal the top N cards of the Villain Deck. You get +1 Attack for each Villain revealed. Put all revealed cards back on top in any order.
- **Danger Sense on Villains** (Black Order): When a Black Order Villain triggers Danger Sense, the +1 Attack per Villain revealed goes to all Black Order Villains in the city AND the Mastermind this turn — not to players.
- **Celestial Boons**: Permanent bonus for the rest of the game, granted when you fight a Celestial. Stays active as long as the Celestial is in your Victory Pile. Multiple boons (including duplicates) all apply.
- **Contest of Champions [CLASS]**: Each player reveals one card from hand or top of deck; their "Contest Score" = that card's printed cost, doubled if it matches the Contest class. Evil reveals 2 cards from the Hero Deck (or 4/6 for some cards) and uses the highest-scoring one. Highest score wins; all others lose. Multicolor contests (e.g., [TECH] or [RANGED]) let any matching class double score.
- **Cosmic Threat** (expanded from F4): Celestials and the Beyonder use this. Celestials add "or [CLASS]" options — you may use either class to reduce Attack, but not both in a single fight. Fighting the same enemy with Cosmic Threat a second time in a turn resets its Attack fully.

### Schemes

---

**The Contest of Champions**
Setup: 11 Twists. Add an extra Hero. Put 11 random cards from the Hero Deck face up in a "Contest Row."
Special Rules: The Contest Row is a face-up row of Hero cards. Each Twist KOs the leftmost card and runs a Contest of Champions for that card's color(s).
Twist 1–4: KO the leftmost card in the Contest Row, then Contest of Champions for that card's color(s). Each player that loses discards a card. If the Mastermind wins, put a Wound next to this Scheme as an "Evil Triumph."
Twist 5–8: Same effect, but in the Contest, Evil selects from 4 cards from the Hero Deck.
Twist 9–11: Same effect, but in the Contest, Evil selects from 6 cards from the Hero Deck.
Evil Wins: When there are 6 Evil Triumphs.

---

**Turn the Soul of Adam Warlock**
Setup: 14 Twists (using 3 Wounds to represent extra Scheme Twists). Put 14 Adam Warlock Hero cards in a face-up stack, ordered from lowest-cost on top to highest-cost on the bottom.
Twist: Set aside the top card of the Adam Warlock stack. This turn you may "Purify" it by spending Attack equal to double its cost. If you do, choose a player to gain that card, then you rescue a Bystander, and you may KO one of your Heroes. If you don't do this by the end of your turn, put that Adam Warlock card into a "Soul's Corruption" stack next to the Scheme.
Evil Wins: When there are 8 Souls Corruptions.

---

**Destroy the Nova Corps**
Setup: 9 Twists. Exactly one Hero must be a Nova Hero. 1 player: 5 Heroes. Each player's starting deck adds 2 Wounds, 1 S.H.I.E.L.D. Officer, and a Nova card that costs 2.
Special Rules: All S.H.I.E.L.D. Officers and Nova Heroes count as "Nova Centurions."
Twist 1–5: Each player must reveal their hand and discard a Nova Centurion. Each player that discarded this way gains a Shard. Each player that didn't discard this way must KO a card from the S.H.I.E.L.D. Officer Stack.
Twist 6–9: Each player must KO a Nova Centurion from the S.H.I.E.L.D. Officer Stack or from their hand or discard pile.
Evil Wins: When there are 5 KO'd Nova Centurions per player.

---

**Annihilation: Conquest**
Setup: 11 Twists. Add an extra Hero.
Twist: Put this Twist next to the Scheme as a "Phalanx Conquest." The highest-cost Hero from the HQ enters the city as a "Phalanx-Infected" Villain.
Special Rules: Each "Phalanx-Infected" Villain has Attack equal to its cost, +1 Attack for each two Phalanx Conquests. If you fight one, choose a player to gain it as a Hero.
Evil Wins: When there are 6 Phalanx-Infected in the city and/or Escape Pile, or the Villain Deck runs out.
